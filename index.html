Financial planning for UK companies entering Switzerland

Enter Switzerland with the numbers understood

Financial planning and implementation support for UK companies entering the Swiss market.

Switzerland can offer UK businesses attractive customers, strong purchasing power and a stable commercial environment. It can also introduce unfamiliar questions around pricing, VAT, local employment, operating structure, currency exposure and the true cost of establishing a presence.

I help UK SMEs turn Swiss expansion plans into financially grounded decisions. Before you form an entity, hire locally or commit substantial capital, I build the financial case, assess the practical operating options and create a clear implementation plan.

From market interest to an investable decision

The management questions nobody else is answering

Your UK accountant knows your existing business. A Swiss fiduciary can manage local filings. A lawyer can establish a company. But someone still needs to answer the management questions:

  • 01 Is Switzerland commercially attractive for this particular business?
  • 02 Can the opportunity support the full cost of entry?
  • 03 Should the company sell from the UK, use a local partner, hire locally or establish a Swiss entity?
  • 04 How should Swiss revenue, costs, cash flow and currency exposure be modelled?
  • 05 What must be resolved before the first contract or local hire?
  • 06 Which advisers are actually required, and at what stage?

That is the role of the Swiss Finance Desk.

The core engagement

Swiss Market Entry Financial Blueprint

A focused financial and operating assessment for UK businesses considering expansion into Switzerland. The engagement typically covers:

  • Swiss-market revenue and pricing scenarios
  • Launch costs and three-year financial projections
  • Cash-flow and working-capital requirements
  • GBP/CHF currency exposure
  • Direct selling, partnership, employment, branch and subsidiary options
  • VAT, payroll, permanent-establishment and compliance questions requiring specialist review
  • Management reporting requirements
  • A 90-day implementation plan
  • A clear go, test, defer or no-go recommendation
